That night Philip couldn’t sleep. He focused incessantly on his pounding heart. He wondered what would happen if it went faster than his body could handle. How could he stop that? And what would come after that?

Philip had just become aware of his own mortality.

He glanced over at a branch and saw his relatives hanging there, sleeping upside down as hummingbirds do. He envied that they were blissfully ignorant of their mortalities. He wished he could go back, but that’s not how life works.

Philip knew he needed to warn them about this, but it would have to wait till morning. He hovered until sunrise.
